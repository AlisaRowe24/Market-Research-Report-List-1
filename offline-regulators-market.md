<p><p>Offline regulators manage voltage and current supply in electronic devices, categorized mainly into Current Mode and Voltage Mode. </p><p>In Current Mode, the regulator adjusts output based on changes in load current, providing rapid response and improved stability, ideal for applications needing precise current control. </p><p>In Voltage Mode, the regulator maintains a constant output voltage, responding to variations in input voltage and load conditions, making it suitable for general power supply applications where voltage stability is prioritized.</p></p>
