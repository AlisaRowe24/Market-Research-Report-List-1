<p><p>The FOUP ID Reader market is segmented into low-frequency and high-frequency RFID reader types. Low-frequency RFID readers operate typically at 125 kHz, offering shorter read ranges and being suitable for close-range applications, such as identifying FOUPs in cleanrooms. High-frequency RFID readers operate around 13.56 MHz, providing longer read ranges and faster data transfer rates, making them ideal for environments requiring swift and accurate inventory management, such as semiconductor manufacturing. Both types enhance efficiency and accuracy in tracking FOUPs.</p></p>
