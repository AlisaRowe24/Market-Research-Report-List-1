<p><p>The gout therapeutics market comprises several key types of medications. Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) provide relief from pain and inflammation during acute attacks. Corticosteroids are used to reduce inflammation and are particularly beneficial in patients who cannot tolerate NSAIDs. Colchicine helps alleviate symptoms and prevent future flare-ups by inhibiting inflammatory processes. Urate-lowering agents, such as allopurinol and febuxostat, work to reduce uric acid levels in the blood, preventing crystal formation and recurrent gout attacks.</p></p>
