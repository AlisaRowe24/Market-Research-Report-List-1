<p><p>The enzyme for baking market consists of various types, each serving a specific function. Amylase breaks down starches into sugars, enhancing fermentation and dough texture. Protease improves dough extensibility and strength by modifying gluten proteins. Cellulase contributes to crumb softness by breaking down cellulose in flour. Oxidase aids in dough oxidation, improving overall quality and shelf life. Other enzymes may include lipases and lactases, which also play roles in flavor enhancement and dough characteristics, catering to diverse baking needs.</p></p>
