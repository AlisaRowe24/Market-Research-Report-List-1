<p><p>The scintillation materials market comprises three main types: solid scintillators, liquid scintillators, and gaseous scintillators. Solid scintillators, typically made from crystals or plastics, are widely used in detectors for radiation measurement due to their durability and efficiency. Liquid scintillators are utilized in applications such as neutrino detection and radiation monitoring, offering high-energy resolution. Gaseous scintillators, often employed in particle physics research, provide flexibility and can be tailored for specific detection requirements. Each type plays a crucial role in radiation detection technologies.</p></p>
