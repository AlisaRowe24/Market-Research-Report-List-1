<p><p>The hydrolyzed milk protein market is primarily divided into two types: whey and casein. Whey protein is derived from the liquid byproduct of cheese production, known for its quick absorption and rich amino acid profile, often used in sports nutrition and supplements. Casein, extracted from curds, digests slowly, providing a sustained release of amino acids, making it popular for meal replacements and nighttime recovery. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs in the nutrition and food sectors.</p></p>
