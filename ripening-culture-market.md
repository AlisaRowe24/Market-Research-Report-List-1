<p><p>Ripening culture markets encompass mesophilic and thermophilic cultures, crucial for cheese production. Mesophilic cultures thrive at moderate temperatures, typically between 20-30°C, fostering flavor and texture development in soft and semi-soft cheeses. In contrast, thermophilic cultures operate at higher temperatures, around 40-55°C, enhancing the production of harder cheeses and promoting faster fermentation. Both markets cater to varying cheese types, supporting dairy manufacturers in meeting consumer demand for diverse flavors, textures, and quality in the cheese industry.</p></p>
