<p><p>Membrane filtration for food and dairy products involves various techniques tailored to specific separation requirements. Reverse osmosis is used to remove dissolved solids and bacteria, enhancing product purity. Ultra-filtration effectively separates larger molecules, such as proteins and fats, making it ideal for milk processing. Nano Filtration allows selective removal of smaller molecules while retaining essential nutrients, often used in juice clarification. Micro Filtration targets bacteria and larger particulates, ensuring microbial safety and extending shelf life. Each method plays a crucial role in product quality and safety in the food industry.</p></p>
