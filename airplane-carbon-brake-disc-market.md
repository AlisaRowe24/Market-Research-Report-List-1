<p><p>The Airplane Carbon Brake Disc Market primarily consists of two types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Short Fiber Impregnated Carbonization (SFIC). CVD involves creating a carbon layer through chemical processes, resulting in high-performance discs with superior thermal stability and lightweight properties. In contrast, SFIC utilizes short carbon fibers embedded in a resin matrix, enhancing strength and heat resistance. Both types are essential for modern aviation, improving braking efficiency and durability, while also contributing to weight reduction in aircraft design.</p></p>
