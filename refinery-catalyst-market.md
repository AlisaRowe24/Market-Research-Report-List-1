<p><p>The refinery catalyst market is segmented into three primary types: FCC (Fluid Catalytic Cracking) catalysts, hydro-processing catalysts, and others. FCC catalysts are essential for converting heavy fractions of crude oil into lighter products like gasoline and diesel through cracking processes. Hydro-processing catalysts facilitate the removal of impurities such as sulfur and nitrogen from hydrocarbon streams, enhancing product quality. The "others" category includes specialty catalysts used in various refining processes, addressing specific reactions and optimization needs within the industry.</p></p>

<p><p>The refinery catalyst market encompasses dispersed and supported catalysts, both essential for enhancing chemical reactions in refining processes. Dispersed catalysts, characterized by their fine distribution within a reaction medium, facilitate higher surface area interaction, improving efficiency. Supported catalysts, on the other hand, involve active catalytic materials anchored on a solid support, optimizing stability and reusability. Together, these catalysts play a critical role in processes such as hydrocracking and catalytic reforming, driving efficiency and yield in petroleum refining operations.</p></p>
