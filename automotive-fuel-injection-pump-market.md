<p><p>The automotive fuel injection pump market primarily features two types: Common Rail Fuel Injection Pumps and Rotary Fuel Injection Pumps. Common Rail Fuel Injection Pumps are used in modern diesel engines, allowing multiple injections per cycle and improving efficiency and performance. In contrast, Rotary Fuel Injection Pumps are typically found in older diesel engines and operate by distributing fuel through rotation, which can lead to less precise fuel delivery. Both types aim to enhance engine performance, fuel efficiency, and emissions control.</p></p>
