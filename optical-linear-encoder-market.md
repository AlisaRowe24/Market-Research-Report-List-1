<p><p>An Optical Linear Encoder is a precision measurement device that converts linear position into digital signals using light. It typically utilizes a light source and a photo-detector, with a scale that features alternating transparent and opaque patterns. When the encoder moves along this scale, the light passing through the patterns is detected, translating the movement into electrical signals that can be processed by a control system. Optical linear encoders are widely employed in applications requiring high accuracy, such as CNC machines, robotics, and automation systems.</p><p>The Optical Linear Encoder Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand for automation across various industries. <p><p>The optical linear encoder market is divided into axle type and shaft type encoders, each serving distinct applications. Axle type encoders are typically used in applications where linear displacement along a fixed path is required, making them ideal for rail systems and conveyor belt tracking. Conversely, shaft type encoders are designed for rotating applications, translating angular position into linear displacement, and are commonly employed in motors and robotics. Both types enhance precision in automation and motion control.</p></p>
