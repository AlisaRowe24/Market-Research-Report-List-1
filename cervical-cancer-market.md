<p><p>The cervical cancer market is categorized into several key diagnostic types. Pap testing, or Pap smears, involves collecting cells from the cervix to identify abnormalities. HPV testing detects the presence of human papillomavirus, a major cause of cervical cancer. Colposcopy is a procedure used to closely examine the cervix after abnormal test results, guiding further evaluation. Cervical biopsies involve taking tissue samples for laboratory analysis to confirm cancer presence. Together, these methods play a crucial role in cervical cancer screening and diagnosis.</p></p>
