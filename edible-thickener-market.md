<p><p>The edible thickener market comprises various types, each serving unique purposes in food production. Animal glue, derived from animal collagen, enhances texture and binding. Vegetable gum, sourced from plant materials, provides thickening and stabilizing properties. Seaweed gum, obtained from algae, offers a natural thickening option rich in fiber. Microbial glue, produced through fermentation, is gaining popularity for its sustainability. Starch, a versatile thickening agent from grains and tubers, is widely used. Other thickeners include proteins and natural extracts, expanding formulation possibilities.</p></p>
