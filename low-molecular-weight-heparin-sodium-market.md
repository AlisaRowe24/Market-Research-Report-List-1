<p><p>Low Molecular Weight Heparin Sodium (LMWH) is an anticoagulant medication derived from heparin, used primarily to prevent and treat blood clots. It is widely prescribed for conditions such as deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, and during surgeries to reduce clotting risks. LMWH is preferred over unfractionated heparin due to its predictable pharmacokinetics, longer half-life, and reduced risk of side effects like heparin-induced thrombocytopenia.</p><p>The Low Molecular Weight Heparin Sodium Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, rising surgical procedures, and growing awareness about thrombotic disorders. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.5% during the forecast period. This growth is further supported by advancements in drug delivery systems and the development of biosimilar versions of LMWH, which are more cost-effective and accessible.</p><p>Key trends in the market include the expansion of LMWH applications in oncology for cancer-associated thrombosis and its use in COVID-19 patients to manage coagulation complications. Additionally, the rise in geriatric populations, who are more susceptible to clotting disorders, is contributing to market expansion. Geographically, North America and Europe dominate the market, while Asia-Pacific is emerging as a high-growth region due to improving healthcare infrastructure and increasing patient awareness.</p></p>
