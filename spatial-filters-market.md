<p><p>Spatial filters are essential devices used to control the spatial characteristics of light. The market offers two primary types: resonant and non-resonant filters. Resonant type filters utilize interference principles, providing precise wavelength selectivity through optical cavity arrangements. They are ideal for applications requiring high sensitivity and narrow bandwidth. In contrast, non-resonant type filters rely on absorption or scattering mechanisms, offering broader wavelength ranges and robustness. Both types serve diverse industries such as telecommunications, imaging, and sensing, catering to specific application needs.</p></p>
