<p><p>Peripheral angiography equipment encompasses various imaging modalities used to visualize blood vessels in the limbs and other peripheral areas. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides detailed soft tissue contrast, while Computed Tomography (CT) offers rapid, high-resolution images of vascular structures. Ultrasound employs sound waves for real-time, non-invasive visualization, making it useful for assessing blood flow. Nuclear imaging utilizes radioactive tracers to evaluate blood flow and perfusion, and X-ray angiography remains a standard technique for visualizing the vascular system. Each type serves unique diagnostic needs in vascular health.</p></p>
